About Nepal

Nepal is situated mainly in the Himalayas and shares borders with China to the north and India to the south, east, and west. The country has a varied landscape that includes fertile plains, subalpine forested hills, and towering mountains. It is home to eight of the world's ten tallest mountains, including Mount Everest (8,848 meters) and Kanchenjunga (8,586 meters). The capital city is Kathmandu, which is also the largest city in the country. There are almost 126 languages spoken by the people following diverse culture and traditions. All the people live in Nepal with harmony and peace. The predominant religions are Hinduism (approximately 81%) and Buddhism (around 9%), making Nepal a significant pilgrimage site for followers of both faiths. The birthplace of Gautama Buddha, Lumbini, is located in southern Nepal.

Festivals in Nepal

Nepal is a culturally diverse country, and its festivals reflect the various ethnic backgrounds and rich religious practices of its people. Religious ceremonies, bright processions, music, dance, and social events are all common features of Nepali festivals, which are widely celebrated with intense zeal and devotion. Major Hindu holidays like Dashain and Tihar, which symbolize the victory of good over evil, include family gatherings, worship of the gods, and animal sacrifices. Buddhists celebrate Buddha Jayanti with peaceful prayers and lighting a candle procession, especially in places like Lumbini and Swayambhunath. Vibrant and rich in ancient traditions, indigenous groups such as the Newars celebrate unique holidays like Indra Jatra and Gai Jatra. These festivities protect Nepal's cultural heritage and encourage harmony among its people.
